Spinor vs Vector Transformations

$${ \begin{array}{llll} \hline \mathbf{Object} & \mathbf{Type} & \mathbf{Exponential \ Form} & \mathbf{Expansion \ Form} \\ \hline \text{Spinor} & \text{Rotation} & \psi' = e^{\frac{\theta}{2}\gamma^1\gamma^2}\psi & \psi' = \left(\cos\frac{\theta}{2} + \gamma^1\gamma^2\sin\frac{\theta}{2}\right)\psi \\ \\ \text{Vector} & \text{Rotation} & V' = e^{\frac{\theta}{2}\gamma^1\gamma^2} V e^{-\frac{\theta}{2}\gamma^1\gamma^2} & V' = (V^x \cos\theta - V^y \sin\theta)\gamma^1 + \dots \\ \\ \text{Spinor} & \text{Boost} & \psi' = e^{\frac{\phi}{2}\gamma^0\gamma^3}\psi & \psi' = \left(\cosh\frac{\phi}{2} + \gamma^0\gamma^3\sinh\frac{\phi}{2}\right)\psi \\ \\ \text{Vector} & \text{Boost} & V' = e^{\frac{\phi}{2}\gamma^0\gamma^3} V e^{-\frac{\phi}{2}\gamma^0\gamma^3} & V'^t = V^t \cosh\phi + V^z \sinh\phi \\ \hline \end{array} }$$

This table contrasts how spinors and vectors transform under rotations and boosts. Spinors transform via a single exponential, while vectors require the adjoint action, leading to more complex transformations. The expansion forms show how the parameters (╬Ė for rotation, Žå for boost) affect the transformations differently for spinors and vectors.

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{Adjoint Action: } & e^{A} V e^{-A} = V + [A, V] + \frac{1}{2!}[A, [A, V]] + \dots \\ \text{For Rotation: } & A = \frac{\theta}{2} \gamma^1\gamma^2 \\ \text{Commutator: } & [A, \gamma^1] = \frac{\theta}{2}(\gamma^1\gamma^2\gamma^1 - \gamma^1\gamma^1\gamma^2) = -\theta\gamma^2 \\ \text{Result: } & V' = V \cos\theta + [(\gamma^1\gamma^2) \cdot V] \sin\theta \end{aligned} $$
